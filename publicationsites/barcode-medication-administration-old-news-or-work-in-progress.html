Barcode Medication Administration: Current State & Future Challenges

Published 10/29/2022 by Barry McClain, PharmD MS | Updated 11/19/2022

Abstract

Topic: Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA): Old News or Work in Progress?

Is Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA) a "solved" problem in healthcare, or is it still a work in progress? This presentation and article argue that despite widespread adoption, the industry has significant ground to cover to reach the ideal of "every patient, every medication, every time."

The content explores the "True North" of BCMAâ€”moving beyond basic compliance to true workflow optimization and safety enhancement (the "five rights"). It provides a critical look at:

  • Technology Gaps: The limitations of 1D barcodes versus the potential of 2D data-rich barcodes (GS1-128) and RFID technology.
  • Adoption Metrics: Insights from Leapfrog Group surveys revealing that many hospitals fail to meet the 95% scanning threshold for both patients and medications.
  • Uncharted Territory: The urgent need to expand BCMA into "horizontal" areas often left behind, such as ambulatory clinics, emergency rooms, and infusion centers.

This resource serves as a call to action for pharmacy informatics leaders to advocate for better vendor solutions, stricter FDA labeling requirements, and a relentless commitment to safety culture.
